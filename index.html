A Note on the Cover

The Yellow-naped Amazon (Amazona auropalliata) is classified as Critically Endangered on the IUCN Red List, threatened by habitat loss and the wild bird trade. Best available estimates place roughly 2,500 adults across its Mesoamerican range, with Costa Rica's population — last surveyed at under 1,000 — among the most rapidly declining. This individual was photographed in the El Oasis garden enjoying a breakfast of cashews.
